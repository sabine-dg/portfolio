About Me

I'm Sabine Dolan-Gaschignard, an undergraduate student studying cartography/GIS and anthropology at the University of Wisconsin - Madison.

Read all about my background and experience.

My passion for archaeology began when I was young and has brought me from Ireland for fieldwork to the Wisconsin DNR as an archaeology assistant, where I paired field methods with cartography and GIS. Today, I apply geospatial tools in the EARTH SENSE lab at UW-Madison, mapping and analyzing changes in Arctic tundra vegetation. I am motivated to explore the natural and cultural processes - both ancient and present - that shape the landscapes we see today.
