Youth Voter Turnout

My visual employs a three-act narrative and storytelling tropes to explore the impact of Election Day Registration (EDR) policies on youth voter turnout and advocates for the nationwide implementation of EDR policies as one way to remove barriers to voting. I was motivated to delve into this topic by my passion for voter rights, especially those of young people. I have worked to engage my peers in civic engagement, specifically by encouraging them to make their voices heard through their votes, and my visual story expands the audience of my efforts. My target audience includes voters and policy makers in the United States, with a particular focus on youth voters (18-24) who consistently have the lowest voter turnout despite holding significant power to determine elections. To investigate EDR impacts on youth voter turnout, I analyzed 2024 presidential election turnout data from the U.S. Census Bureau and EDR policy information from Ballotpedia.
